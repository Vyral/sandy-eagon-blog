Our warm weather is winding down here, but one of the things I really enjoy about both summer and autumn is that both seasons give us a chance to get outside and play golf. It’s a relatively new hobby of mine, but I’ve had a really good time strengthening my game lately and thinking about how golf is like real estate.

I’m kind of a project person, and golf has been a different experience for me because it’s different than my previous hobbies like quilting, gardening, or scrapbooking, where I could produce something tangible to show for myself. In golf, when I have a great score, I still feel like I’ve accomplished something, though.

How does this relate to real estate? Real estate has an end product as well. Most of the time, you have a closing at the end—someone bought a house and someone sold a house. Whether you have a paycheck, a new home, or something off your shoulders that you don’t have to think about anymore, it’s a great thing to look back on.



“
In both golf and real estate, I can depend on feedback in order to get better.

”

 
When I’m golfing and I make a mistake, I can ask for feedback from the people in my group to advise me on what I did wrong. In real estate, if an issue comes up, I have an amazing team that gives me feedback in order to help me determine the best way to serve the client. It’s very much a group effort, but I still have something great to show for it at the end.

As you may have seen from my Facebook post recently, I have decided to be on the budget model of golf. This doesn’t mean it will cost less money; it means I am simply getting every last pennies’ worth of value because I have so many strokes out there during the course of each game. I’m going to enjoy it, appreciate the people around me, and learn from my mistakes.

I was also fortunate enough to have been able to join golf and real estate in a literal sense when I sponsored a charity golf tournament this last summer for Leah’s Dream Foundation. Whenever I can put those two things together, I get the chance to know people, serve them, and have a great time. So, if I ever see you out on the golf course, don’t hesitate to come up and say a quick hello.

In the meantime, if you’re looking to buy or sell real estate, don’t hesitate to give me a call or shoot me an email. I look forward to hearing from you!
